Blood Donor Eligibility Criteria

Thank you for considering becoming a blood donor! Below are the essential criteria to register as a donor:

  • Be in good general health and feeling well
  • Be at least 18 to 60 years old
  • Weigh more than 100 pounds (45 kg)
  • Donor profile will not be visible until 120 days have passed since the last blood donation.
  • Bring a list of medications you are taking
  • Temporary deferral after recent surgery or tattoos due to infection risk
  • Additional weight requirements for donors under 18 years old and high school donors
  • Temporary deferral due to recent travel to high-risk infectious disease regions
  • Hemoglobin range of 12.5 to 17 g/dL for males and 12 to 16.5 g/dL for females
  • No recent infections or illnesses within the last X days
  • Temporary deferral due to recent vaccinations
  • Donors having a history of any of the following within the previous one year must be deferred for another one year:
    • Jaundice
    • Blood transfusion
    • Major surgery
    • Invasive dental treatment
  • Donors having a history of/suffering from any of the following must be permanently deferred:
    • Hepatitis B
    • Hepatitis C
    • HIV
    • Syphilis
    • Cardiovascular diseases
    • Central nervous system diseases
    • Malignancy
    • Insulin-dependent diabetes mellitus
    • Chronic respiratory diseases
    • Chronic renal diseases

For any queries or clarification regarding eligibility, feel free to contact us.
